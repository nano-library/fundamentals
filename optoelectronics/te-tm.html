Transverse Electric (TE) and Magnetic (TM) Modes

The guided modes of a planar waveguide are classified according to the presence or absence of longitudinal field components:

  • TE Modes (Transverse Electric):
    \[ E_z = 0 \] The electric field lies entirely in the transverse plane.
  • TM Modes (Transverse Magnetic):
    \[ H_z = 0 \] The magnetic field lies entirely in the transverse plane.

This separation works because:

  • The refractive index varies only with x.
  • The structure is invariant along z.
  • The medium is isotropic.

Under these conditions, Maxwell's equations mathematically decouple into two independent scalar wave equations, one for TE and one for TM.

Instead of solving six coupled vector equations, we reduce the problem to:

  • One scalar equation for TE modes
  • One scalar equation for TM modes

That is the backbone of planar waveguide theory.

A specific electromagnetic field distribution that satisfies Maxwell's equations and the boundary conditions of the waveguide, and propagates along the guide with a definite propagation constant.
