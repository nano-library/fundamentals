Wave Guide

An optical waveguide is a structure that guides electromagnetic waves by restricting the transmission of energy primarily to one direction.

What “guiding” really means:

  • The field is confined in the transverse directions (say x and y).
  • The energy flow is mainly along one axis (say z).

Waveguiding is achieved by spatial confinement of the electromagnetic field using either

  • Total Internal Reflection (TIR): confinement by repeated internal reflection at an interface, because the wave cannot refract out.
  • Photonic Band Gap (PBG): confinement because the cladding is engineered to forbid propagation for certain frequencies (a dielectric “crystal wall”).

TOTAL INTERNAL REFLECTION (TIR) GUIDANCE

If the core has higher refractive index than cladding, the wave inside the core hits the boundary and cannot escape when the incidence angle is large enough.

Conditions for TIR guiding:

  • Index condition: \(\eta_{\text{core}} > \eta_{cladding}\).
  • Index profile: step-index or graded-index (you need a meaningful “core” region).
  • Angle condition: incidence angle at the core-cladding boundary must exceed the critical angle.

Critical angle:

\[ \theta_c = sin^{-1}{(\frac{n_2}{n_1})} \]

TIR required

\[ \theta > \theta_c \]

PHOTONIC BAND GAP (PBG) GUIDANCE

Here guiding does not need \(\eta_{\text{core}} > \eta_{cladding}\).

  • The cladding is a periodic dielectric structure.
  • That periodicity creates a photonic band gap: frequency ranges that cannot propagate in the cladding.

OPTICAL FIBERS

A fiber is a cylindrical dielectric waveguide with:

  • a central core (higher index for TIR fibers),
  • surrounded by cladding (lower index),
  • so light zig-zags (or more accurately, forms guided modes) along the axis.

PLANAR OPTICAL WAVEGUIDES

The simplest optical waveguides are planar waveguides. A planar waveguide consists of a thin dielectric film (core) sandwiched between two materials having lower refractive indices (cladding).

Planar waveguides form the basic building blocks of integrated optics and are widely used for analyzing wave propagation in guided structures.
