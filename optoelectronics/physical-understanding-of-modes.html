Physical Understanding of Modes

PHYSICAL UNDERSTANDING OF MODES

Consider the electric-field pattern inside the film core \(\left(-\dfrac{d}{2} < x < \dfrac{d}{2}\right)\).

For a symmetric TE mode (even in \(x\)):

\[ E_y(x)=A\cos(\kappa x). \]

So the complete (time-harmonic) field in the film is:

\[ E_y(x,z,t)=A\cos(\kappa x)\,e^{i(\omega t-\beta z)}. \]

Using \(\cos(\kappa x)=\dfrac{1}{2}\left(e^{i\kappa x}+e^{-i\kappa x}\right)\), this is a superposition of two plane waves:

\[ E_y=\frac{A}{2}e^{i(\omega t-\beta z+\kappa x)}+\frac{A}{2}e^{i(\omega t-\beta z-\kappa x)}. \]

A general plane wave has the phase factor:

\[ e^{i(\omega t-k_x x-k_y y-k_z z)}. \]

which represents propagation along the direction of the wavevector \(\mathbf{k}\), whose components are \(k_x,k_y,k_z\).

Comparing terms, for the two constituent plane waves in the film core:

\[ k_x=\pm \kappa,\qquad k_y=0,\qquad k_z=\beta. \]

The ray angle \(\theta\) inside the film (measured from the \(z\)-axis) satisfies:

\[ \tan\theta=\frac{|k_x|}{k_z}=\frac{\kappa}{\beta}. \]

Also, in the film core \(|\mathbf{k}|=k_1=k_0 n_1\), and

\[ k_1^2=k_x^2+k_z^2=\kappa^2+\beta^2 \] \[ \cos\theta=\frac{\beta}{k_0 n_1},\;\;\sin\theta=\frac{\kappa}{k_0 n_1}. \]

Hence:

\[ \beta=k_0 n_1\cos\theta. \]

Guided-mode condition (effective index between cladding and core):

\[ n_2<\frac{\beta}{k_0}< n_1. \]
