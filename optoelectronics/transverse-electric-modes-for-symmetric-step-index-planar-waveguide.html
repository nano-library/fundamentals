Transverse Electric (TE) Modes for Symmetric Step-index Planar Waveguide

A symmetric planar (slab) waveguide consists of a film (core) of thickness \(d\) and refractive index \(n_1\), bounded by cladding of refractive index \(n_2\), with \(n_1>n_2\).

\[ n(x)= \begin{cases} n_1, & |x|<\dfrac{d}{2},\\[6pt] n_2, & |x|>\dfrac{d}{2}. \end{cases} \]

Propagation is along the \(z\)-direction with propagation constant \(\beta\). For time-harmonic fields, assume

\[ E_y(x,z,t)=E_y(x)\,e^{i(\beta z-\omega t)}. \]

TRANSVERSE ELECTRIC (TE) MODES

For TE modes (with respect to the direction of propagation \(z\)):

\[ E_z=0,\qquad E_y\neq 0. \]

The associated magnetic-field components follow from Maxwell’s equations:

\[ H_x=-\frac{\beta}{\omega\mu_0}\,E_y, \qquad H_z=\frac{i}{\omega\mu_0}\,\frac{dE_y}{dx}. \]

Substitution into Maxwell's equations yields the scalar wave equation for \(E_y(x)\):

\[ \frac{d^2E_y}{dx^2}+\left(k_0^2 n^2(x)-\beta^2\right)E_y=0, \qquad k_0=\frac{\omega}{c}. \]

GUIDED MODE CONDITION

For guided modes the field must be oscillatory in the core and evanescent in the cladding. Hence

\[ k_0 n_2< \beta< k_0 n_1, \qquad\text{or}\qquad n_2< \frac{\beta}{k_0}< n_1. \]

SOLUTIONS TO EACH REGION

Core Region \(\left(|x|<\dfrac{d}{2}\right)\)

\[ \frac{d^2E_y}{dx^2}+\kappa^2 E_y=0, \qquad \kappa^2=k_0^2 n_1^2-\beta^2. \] \[ E_y(x)=A\cos(\kappa x)+B\sin(\kappa x). \]

CLADDING REGION \(\left(|x|>\dfrac{d}{2}\right)\)

\[ \frac{d^2E_y}{dx^2}-\gamma^2 E_y=0, \qquad \gamma^2=\beta^2-k_0^2 n_2^2. \]

Physical boundedness requires an exponential decay away from the film:

\[ E_y(x)= \begin{cases} C\,e^{-\gamma(x-d/2)}, & x>\dfrac{d}{2},\\[6pt] C\,e^{+\gamma(x+d/2)}, & x<-\dfrac{d}{2}. \end{cases} \]

SYMMETRY OF MODES

Since \(n(x)=n(-x)\), modes can be classified by parity:

  • Even (symmetric): \(E_y(-x)=E_y(x)\) \(\Rightarrow\) core field is cosine-like.
  • Odd (antisymmetric): \(E_y(-x)=-E_y(x)\) \(\Rightarrow\) core field is sine-like.

Thus one may take:

\[ \text{Even:}\quad E_y(x)=A\cos(\kappa x), \qquad \text{Odd:}\quad E_y(x)=B\sin(\kappa x), \quad (|x| < d/2). \]

BOUNDARY CONDITION (TE)

At the interfaces \(x=\pm d/2\), tangential field components must be continuous. For TE modes this gives:

  • \(E_y\) is continuous,
  • \(\dfrac{dE_y}{dx}\) is continuous.

EIGENVALUE EQUATIONS (Dispersion) EQUATIONS

Applying the boundary conditions yields the TE mode eigenvalue equations:

Even (Symmetric) TE Modes

\[ \kappa \tan\left(\frac{\kappa d}{2}\right)=\gamma. \]

Odd (Antisymmetric) TE Modes

\[ -\kappa \cot\left(\frac{\kappa d}{2}\right)=\gamma. \]

Only discrete values of \(\beta\) satisfy these transcendental equations; each allowed \(\beta\) corresponds to one guided TE mode.

NORMALIZED FREQUENCY PARAMETER

The normalized frequency (or \(V\)-parameter) is defined by

\[ V=k_0 d\sqrt{n_1^2-n_2^2}. \]

where, \(k_0 = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda}\).

\(V\) governs the number of guided modes supported by the slab.
