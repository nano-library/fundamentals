Optical Directional Coupler

Coupling Factors

  • Separation between waveguides
  • Propagation constants
  • Operating wavelength

Consider two independent waveguides supporting modes \(a(z)\) and \(b(z)\).

For waveguide 1:

\[ E_y(x,z)\propto e^{-i\beta_1 z} \] \[ \frac{da}{dz}=-i\beta_1 a \tag{1} \]

For waveguide 2:

\[ \frac{db}{dz}=-i\beta_2 b \tag{2} \]

COUPLED MODE EQUATIONS

When the waveguides are brought close, evanescent fields overlap and coupling occurs:

\[ \frac{da}{dz}=-i\beta_1 a - i\kappa_{12} b \tag{3} \] \[ \frac{db}{dz}=-i\beta_2 b - i\kappa_{21} a \tag{4} \]

\(\kappa_{12},\kappa_{21}\) are coupling coefficients. For identical waveguides: \(\kappa_{12}=\kappa_{21}=\kappa\).

Eigenmode Solution

Assume solutions of the form: \[ a(z)=a_0 e^{-i\beta z}, \qquad b(z)=b_0 e^{-i\beta z} \tag{5,6} \] Substitution into (3) and (4) gives: \[ a_0(\beta-\beta_1)-\kappa_{12} b_0=0 \tag{7} \] \[ b_0(\beta-\beta_2)-\kappa_{21} a_0=0 \tag{8} \] For non-trivial solutions: \[ \beta^2-\beta(\beta_1+\beta_2)+(\beta_1\beta_2-\kappa^2)=0 \tag{9} \] where \[ \kappa=\sqrt{\kappa_{12}\kappa_{21}}. \] Thus the propagation constants of the symmetric and antisymmetric supermodes are: \[ \beta_{s,a}=\frac{\beta_1+\beta_2}{2} \pm \sqrt{\frac{(\beta_1-\beta_2)^2}{4}+\kappa^2} \tag{10} \]

Identical Waveguides

If \(\beta_1=\beta_2=\beta_0\): \[ \beta_s=\beta_0+\kappa \tag{13} \] \[ \beta_a=\beta_0-\kappa \tag{14} \]

Power Exchange

For excitation in waveguide 1 at \(z=0\): \[ |a(z)|^2= 1-\frac{\kappa^2}{\frac{\Delta\beta^2}{4}+\kappa^2} \sin^2\!\left( \sqrt{\frac{\Delta\beta^2}{4}+\kappa^2}\; z \right) \tag{18} \] \[ |b(z)|^2= \frac{\kappa^2}{\frac{\Delta\beta^2}{4}+\kappa^2} \sin^2\!\left( \sqrt{\frac{\Delta\beta^2}{4}+\kappa^2}\; z \right) \tag{19} \] where \[ \Delta\beta=\beta_1-\beta_2. \] Power conservation: \[ |a(z)|^2+|b(z)|^2=1. \]

Modulation Length

\[ h=\frac{2\pi}{2\sqrt{\frac{\Delta\beta^2}{4}+\kappa^2}} \]

Coupling Length

Minimum length for maximum power transfer: \[ L_c=\frac{h}{2} = \frac{\pi}{2\sqrt{\frac{\Delta\beta^2}{4}+\kappa^2}} \] For identical waveguides (\(\Delta\beta=0\)): \[ L_c=\frac{\pi}{2\kappa} \]
