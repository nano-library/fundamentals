Gaussian Elimination Method

In Gaussian elimination method, we first perform a sequence of elementary row operations to get a system with upper triangular coefficient matrix.

The general Gaussian elimination procedure applied to the linear system is handled in the following manner.

\[ a_{11}^{(1)}x_1 + a_{12}^{(1)}x_2 + \ldots + a_{1n}^{(1)}x_n = b_1^{(1)}, \tag{\(E_1\)} \\ \] \[ a_{21}^{(1)}x_1 + a_{22}^{(1)}x_2 + \ldots + a_{2n}^{(1)}x_n = b_2^{(1)}, \tag{\(E_2\)} \] \[ \vdots \] \[ a_{n1}^{(1)}x_1 + a_{n2}^{(1)}x_2 + \ldots + a_{nn}^{(1)}x_n = b_n^{(1)}, \tag{\(E_n\)} \]

The coefficients \(a_{i,i}^{i}\) belong to system (1)

That is, we convert the equation \(AX = b\) to \(\mathcal{U}x = b'\) where \(\mathcal{U}\) is upper triangular. This process is known as forward elimination.

we have assumed \(a^{(i)}_{i,i} \neq 0\) in this method.

On the \(k^{th}\) (\(k\) runs from \(1\) to \(n - 1\)) step we do the following:

We eliminate \(x_k\) from all the equations from \(E_k + 1\) to \(E_n\).

To do this we first calculate the multipliers

\[ m_{i, k} = \frac{a_{i,k}^{(k)}}{a_{k,k}^{(k)}}, \quad i = k+1,\ldots,n. \]

Next we perform the operation

\[ E_i = E_i =- m_{i,k}E_k, \quad k+1,\ldots,n. \]

The updated coefficients are then defined as

\[ a_{i,j}^{(k+1)} = a_{i,j}^{(k)} - m_{i,k}a_{k,j}^{(k)}, \quad i,j = k+1,\ldots,n. \] \[ b_{i}^{(k+1)} = b_{i}^{(k)} - m_{i,k}b_{k}^{(k)}, \quad i = k+1,\ldots,n. \]

After repeating this step for \(k=1(1)n-1\) we get the following system,

\[ A^{(n)} x = b^{(n)} \]

where \(A^{(n)} = (a^{(i)}_{i,j})\) is upper triangular.

We then apply the so called back substitution method to get the solution.

\[ x_n = \frac{b^{(n)_n}}{a^{(n)_{n,n}}}, \] \[ x_i = \frac{b_i^{(i)} - \sum_{j=i+1}^{n} a_{i,j}^{(i)}x_{j}}{a_{i,i}^{(i)}}, \quad i = n-1, n-2,\ldots,1. \]

Example 1:

Solve the following system using Gaussian elimination:

\[ \begin{aligned} 2x - y + 3z &= 9 \\ x + y + z &= 6 \\ 3x + 4y - 2z &= 5 \end{aligned} \]

Solution:

We first calculate the multipliers:

\[ m_{i, k} = \frac{a_{i,k}^{(k)}}{a_{k,k}^{(k)}}, \quad i = k+1,\ldots,n. \] \[ m_{2,1} = \frac{a^{(1)}_{2,1}}{a^{(1)}_{1,1}} = \frac{1}{2} \] \[ m_{3,1} = \frac{a^{(1)}_{3,1}}{a^{(1)}_{1,1}} = \frac{3}{2} \]

Now, we perform the row operations:

\[ R_1 \rightarrow R_2 - m_{2,1}R_1, \quad R_3 \rightarrow R_3 - m_{3,1}R_1 \]

Which is

\[ R_1 \rightarrow R_2 - \frac{1}{2}R_1, \quad R_3 \rightarrow R_3 - \frac{3}{2}R_1 \]

Then we get,

\[ \begin{aligned} 2x - y + 3z &= 9 \\ 0 + \frac{3}{2}y - \frac{1}{2} z &= \frac{3}{2} \\ 0 + \frac{11}{2}y - \frac{13}{2}z &= -\frac{17}{2} \end{aligned} \]

We calculate the multipliers again:

\[ m_{i, k} = \frac{a_{i,k}^{(k)}}{a_{k,k}^{(k)}}, \quad i = k+1,\ldots,n. \] \[ m_{3,2} = \frac{a^{(2)}_{3,2}}{a^{(1)}_{2,2}} = \frac{\frac{11}{2}}{\frac{3}{2}} = \frac{11}{3} \]

Then, we perform the row operations again:

\[ R_3 \rightarrow R_3 - \frac{11}{3}R_1 \]

Now, we get

\[ \begin{aligned} 2x - y + 3z &= 9 \\ 0 + \frac{3}{2}y - \frac{1}{2} z &= \frac{3}{2} \\ 0 + 0 - \frac{14}{3}z &= -14 \end{aligned} \]

This is upper-triangular system.

\[ \begin{aligned} 2x - y + 3z &= 9 \\ \frac{3}{2}y - \frac{1}{2} z &= \frac{3}{2} \\ - \frac{14}{3}z &= -14 \end{aligned} \]

We then apply the so called back substitution method to get the solution.

From the last equation,

\[ - \frac{14}{3}z = -14 \implies z = \frac{42}{14} = 3 \]

Substituting \(z = 3\) in second equation,

\[ \frac{3}{2}y - \frac{1}{2} z = \frac{3}{2} \implies \frac{3}{2}y - \frac{1}{2} (3) = \frac{3}{2} \] \[ y - 1 = 1 \implies y = 2 \]

Substituting \(z = 3\) and \(y = 2\) in first equation,

\[ 2x - y + 3z = 9 \implies 2x - (2) + 3(3) = 9 \] \[ 2x - 2 + 9 = 9 \implies x = \frac{2}{2} = 1 \]

Solution

\[ (x,y,z) = (1,2,3) \]
