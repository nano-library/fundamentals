Cholesky's Decomposition

\(LDV\) Decomposition

Consider

\[ U = \begin{bmatrix} u_{11} & u_{12} \\ 0 & u_{22} \end{bmatrix}, \quad |U| = u_{11}u_{22} \]

Let

\[ D = \begin{bmatrix} u_{11} & 0 \\ 0 & u_{22} \end{bmatrix}, \quad |D| = u_{11}u_{22} \] \[ V = D^{-1}U \] \[ V = \frac{1}{u_{11}u_{22}} \begin{bmatrix} u_{22} & 0 \\ 0 & u_{11} \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} u_{11} & u_{12} \\ 0 & u_{22} \end{bmatrix} \] \[ V = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & \frac{u_{12}}{u_{11}} \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \] \[ V = D^{-1}U \] \[ U = DV \] \[ A = LU = LDV \]

where

\(L\) is a unit lower trianglar

\(D\) is a diagonal matrix

\(V\) is a unit upper trianglar

Case 1

If \(A\) is symmetric, \(A = A^{-1}\)

\[ LDV = (LDV)^{T} \] \[ LDV = V^TD^TL^T \] \[ \boxed{LDV = V^TDL^T} \] \[ L = V^T \quad \text{and} \quad V = L^T \] \[ \boxed{A = LDV = LDL^T = V^TDV} \] Case 2

If \(A\) is positive definite

\[ Ax = \lambda x, \quad x \ne 0 \] \[ x^TAx > 0, \quad x = \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix} \] \[ x^TAx = x^T(\lambda x) = \lambda x^T x = \lambda \sum_{i=1}^n x_i^2 \] \[ \lambda_i > 0, \quad i =1,2,\ldots, n \]

From case 1 and case 2

\[ A = LDL^T, \quad d_{ii}, \quad i=1,2,\ldots,n \]

Define

\[ D^{\frac{1}{2}} = \begin{bmatrix} \sqrt{d_{11}} & \ldots & 0 \\ 0 & \sqrt{d_{22}} & 0 \\ \ddots \\ 0 & \ldots & \sqrt{d_{nn}} \end{bmatrix}_{n \times n} \]

such that

\[ D^{\frac{1}{2}}D^{\frac{1}{2}} = D \] \[ A = LDL^T = LD^{\frac{1}{2}}D^{\frac{1}{2}}L^T \]

Let

\[ G = LD^{\frac{1}{2}} \] \[ G^T = (LD^{\frac{1}{2}})^T = D^{\frac{1}{2}}L^T \] \[ \boxed{A = GG^T} \]

This is Cholesky's decomposition.

Note:

If all the principal minors are non-zero. The \(A\) is positive definite.
