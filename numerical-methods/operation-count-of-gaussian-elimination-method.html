Operation Count of Gaussian Elimination Method

he following table gives the exact operation counts for forward elimination and back substitution when solving an \(n \times n\) linear system using Gaussian elimination (without pivoting).

The total counts of operations in Gaussian elimination method.

Multiplications / Divisions Additions / Subtractions
Forward elimination \(\frac{2n^3 + 3n^2 - 5n}{6}\) \(\frac{n^3 - n}{3}\)
Back substitution \(\frac{n^2 + n}{2}\) \(\frac{n^2 - n}{2}\)
Total \(\frac{n^3}{3} + n^2 - \frac{n}{3}\) \(\frac{n^3}{3} + \frac{n^2}{2} - \frac{5n}{6}\)

Hence from above Table, Gaussian elimination method requires about \(\frac{2}{3}n^3 + O(n^2)\) arithmetic operations.

Forward elimination

Multiplication/Division

On the \(k^{th}\) step in below equation, we calculate \((n - k)\) divisions.

\[ m_{i, k} = \frac{a_{i,k}^{(k)}}{a_{k,k}^{(k)}}, \quad i = k+1,\ldots,n. \]

Also in below equation, we have \((n - k)(n - k)\) multiplications.

\[ a_{i,j}^{(k+1)} = a_{i,j}^{(k)} - m_{i,k}a_{k,j}^{(k)}, \quad i,j = k+1,\ldots,n. \]

In below equation, we have \((n - k)\) multiplications.

\[ b_{i}^{(k+1)} = b_{i}^{(k)} - m_{i,k}b_{k}^{(k)}, \quad i = k+1,\ldots,n. \]

So, in total, we have

\[ (n - k) + (n - k)(n - k) + (n - k) = (n - k)^2 + 2(n - k) \]

multiplications/divisions on the \(k^{th}\) step.

As \(k\) is from \(1\) to \(n - 1\) the total number of multiplications/divisions in forward elimination is given by,

\[ \sum_{k=1}^{n-1} \left( (n-k)^2 + 2(n-k)\right) = \frac{2n^3 + 3n^2 - 5n}{6} \]

In detail,

\[ \begin{aligned} \sum_{k=1}^{n-1} \left( (n-k)^2 + 2(n-k) \right) &= \sum_{k=1}^{n-1} (n-k)^2 + 2\sum_{k=1}^{n-1} (n-k) \\ &= \sum_{k=1}^{n-1} k^2 + 2\sum_{k=1}^{n-1} k \\ &= \frac{(n-1)n(2n-1)}{6} + 2\frac{(n-1)n}{2} \\ &= \frac{2n^3 + 3n^2 - 5n}{6} \end{aligned} \]
Addition

In the below equation, we have \((n - k)(n - k)\) subtractions

\[ a_{i,j}^{(k+1)} = a_{i,j}^{(k)} - m_{i,k}a_{k,j}^{(k)}, \quad i,j = k+1,\ldots,n. \]

In the below equation, we have \((n - k)\) subtractions

\[ b_{i}^{(k+1)} = b_{i}^{(k)} - m_{i,k}b_{k}^{(k)}, \quad i = k+1,\ldots,n. \]

So, in total, we have

\[ (n - k)(n - k) + (n - k) = (n - k)^2 + (n - k) \]

subtractions on the \(k^{th}\) step.

Therefore, the total number of additions/subtractions in forward elimination is

\[ \sum_{k=1}^{n-1} \left( (n-k)^2 + (n-k) \right) = \frac{n^3 - n}{3} \]

In detail,

\[ \begin{aligned} \sum_{k=1}^{n-1} \left( (n-k)^2 + (n-k) \right) &= \sum_{k=1}^{n-1} (n-k)^2 + \sum_{k=1}^{n-1} (n-k) \\ &= \sum_{k=1}^{n-1} k^2 + \sum_{k=1}^{n-1} k \\ &= \frac{(n-1)n(2n-1)}{6} + \frac{(n-1)n}{2} \\ &= \frac{n^3 - n}{3} \end{aligned} \]

Back elimination

Multiplication/Division

In the below equation, we have \(1\) division

\[ x_n = \frac{b^{(n)_n}}{a^{(n)_{n,n}}} \]

In the below equation, we have \((n - i)\) multiplications and \(1\) division.

\[ x_i = \frac{b_i^{(i)} - \sum_{j=i+1}^{n} a_{i,j}^{(i)}x_{j}}{a_{i,i}^{(i)}}, \quad i = n-1, n-2,\ldots,1. \]

As \(i = 1 ( 1 ) n - 1\), total multiplications/divisions in back substitution is

\[ 1 + \sum_{i=1}^{n-1} (n - i + 1) = \frac{n^2 + n}{2} \]

In detail,

\[ \begin{aligned} 1 + \sum_{i=1}^{n-1} (n - i + 1) &= 1 + \sum_{i=1}^{n-1} (n - i) + (n - 1) \\ &= n + \sum_{i=1}^{n-1} (n - i) \\ &= n + \frac{(n-1)n}{2} \\ &= \frac{n^2 + n}{2}. \end{aligned} \]
Addition/Subtraction

In the below equation, we have \((n - i-1)\) additions and \(1\) subtraction.

\[ x_i = \frac{b_i^{(i)} - \sum_{j=i+1}^{n} a_{i,j}^{(i)}x_{j}}{a_{i,i}^{(i)}}, \quad i = n-1, n-2,\ldots,1. \]

As \(i = 1 ( 1 ) n - 1\), the total number of additions/subtractions is

\[ \sum_{i=1}^{n-1} \big((n - i - 1) + 1\big) = \sum_{i=1}^{n-1} (n - i) = \frac{(n-1)n}{2} = \frac{n^2 - n}{2} \]
