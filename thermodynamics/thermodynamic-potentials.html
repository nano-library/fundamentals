Thermodynamic Potentials

Thermodynamic Potentials: Enthalpy (H), Helmholtz Free Energy (F), and Gibbs Free Energy (G)

1. Enthalpy (H)

Definition:

H = U + PV

Where:

  • U = Internal Energy
  • P = Pressure
  • V = Volume

Concept:
Enthalpy represents the total heat content of a system at constant pressure.

Key Relation (at constant pressure):
ΔH = qp

The change in enthalpy equals the heat absorbed or released at constant pressure.

2. Helmholtz Free Energy (F)

Definition:

F = U − TS

Where:

  • T = Temperature
  • S = Entropy

Concept:
Helmholtz free energy represents the maximum useful work obtainable from a system at constant temperature and volume.

Equilibrium Condition (constant T and V):
ΔF ≤ 0

The system minimizes F at equilibrium.

3. Gibbs Free Energy (G)

Definition:

G = H − TS

or

G = U + PV − TS

Concept:
Gibbs free energy represents the maximum non-expansion work obtainable at constant temperature and pressure.

Spontaneity Criterion (constant T and P):

  • ΔG < 0 → Spontaneous process
  • ΔG = 0 → System at equilibrium
  • ΔG > 0 → Non-spontaneous process

Summary
