Laws of Thermodynamics

Zeroth Law of Thermodynamics

If a system \(A\) is in thermal equilibrium with system \(C\) and a system \(B\) is also in thermal equilibrium with system \(C\), then \(A\) and \(B\) will be in thermal equilibrium with each other.

First Law of Thermodynamics

The change in internal energy \(U\) for any transition between two equilibrium states is given by $$ \Delta U = Q - W $$ $$ \text{or} $$ $$ dU = dQ - dW $$ Where \(Q\) is heat exchanged,

\(W\) is work done by the system if \(W > 0\) and W is work done on the system if \(W < 0\).

Assumptions: No friction or loss

Second Law of Thermodynamics

Clausius statement of second law

Heat never flows spontaneously from a colder object to a hotter object.

Kelvin statement of second law

It is impossible to convert the heat from a single source into work without any other effect.

Planck statement of second law (Entropy statement)

The entropy of an isolated system/closed system never decreases, it remains constant for reversible process and increases for irreversible process.
