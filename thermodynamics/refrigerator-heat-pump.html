Refrigerator and Heat Pump

Refrigerator

A refrigerator removes heat from a cold reservoir and rejects it to a hot reservoir by using external work. The heat removed from the cold reservoir is \(Q_c\).

Coefficient of Performance (COP) of Refrigerator
\[ k_R = \frac{Q_c}{W} \]

Since for a complete cycle \(W = Q_h - Q_c\),

\[ k_R = \frac{Q_c}{Q_h - Q_c}. \]

Heat Pump

A heat pump delivers heat to a hot reservoir. It is “the same machine,” just judged by what you care about: a house wants \(Q_h\), not \(Q_c\).

Coefficient of Performance (COP) of Heat Pump
\[ k_P = \frac{Q_h}{W} \]

Using \(W = Q_h - Q_c\),

\[ k_P = \frac{Q_h}{Q_h - Q_c}. \]
Useful Relation Between COPs
\[ k_P = \frac{Q_h}{W} = \frac{Q_c + W}{W} = \frac{Q_c}{W} + 1 = k_R + 1. \]

Problem 1

A heat engine has efficiency \(e = 35\% = 0.35\) and average power output \(P = 4\,\text{kW} = 4000\,\text{W}\). Find (i) average work done in 1 minute, (ii) minimum heat discharged to air in 1 minute.

(i) Average work done in 1 minute
\[ W = P\,t = 4000 \times 60 = 240000\,\text{J} = 240\,\text{kJ}. \]
(ii) Minimum heat discharged in 1 minute

Efficiency is \(e = \dfrac{W}{Q_h}\Rightarrow Q_h = \dfrac{W}{e}\). Then heat rejected is \(Q_c = Q_h - W\).

\[ Q_h = \frac{240000}{0.35} = 685714.2857\,\text{J} \approx 6.857\times 10^5\,\text{J}. \] \[ Q_c = Q_h - W = 685714.2857 - 240000 = 445714.2857\,\text{J} \approx 4.457\times 10^5\,\text{J}. \]

So the minimum heat discharged to air in 1 minute is \(\boxed{Q_c \approx 4.46\times 10^5\,\text{J} \;(\approx 446\,\text{kJ})}\).
