Equipartition Theorem

The \textit{equipartition theorem} states that, in thermal equilibrium, each independent quadratic degree of freedom contributes an average energy of \[ \frac{1}{2}k_B T \] to the total energy of a system, where \(k_B\) is the Boltzmann constant and \(T\) is the absolute temperature. In a gas or many-particle system, degrees of freedom arise from different forms of energy, such as:

- Translational kinetic energy,

- Rotational kinetic energy,

- Potential energy (when it appears quadratically in the Hamiltonian).
