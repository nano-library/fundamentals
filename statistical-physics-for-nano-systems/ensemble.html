1. Ensemble

Consider, at a single instant of time, a large number of systems which are characterized by the same macrostate as the original system but are, naturally enough, in all sorts of possible microstates. Then, under ordinary circumstances, we may expect that the average behavior of any system in this collection, which we call an ensemble, would be identical to the time-averaged behavior of the given system.
