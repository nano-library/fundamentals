Mean of a Distribution

Let \( u \) be a parameter that takes values \( u_1, u_2, \ldots, u_N \) with corresponding probabilities \( p(u_1), p(u_2), \ldots, p(u_N) \).

The mean (or expectation value) of the distribution is defined as

\[ \bar{u} = \frac{\displaystyle \sum_{i=1}^{N} u_i \, p(u_i)} {\displaystyle \sum_{i=1}^{N} p(u_i)} \]

If the probabilities are normalized, i.e.,

\[ \sum_{i=1}^{N} p(u_i) = 1, \]

then the mean reduces to

\[ \bar{u} = \sum_{i=1}^{N} u_i \, p(u_i). \]

Second Moment (Variance)

The second moment about the mean, also called the variance, is

\[ (\Delta u)^2 = \sum_{i=1}^{N} p(u_i) \left( u_i - \bar{u} \right)^2, \qquad (\ge 0) \]

This quantity is always non-negative.

Mean of a Function

Let \( f(u) \) be a function of the variable \( u \). Then the mean value of \( f(u) \) is

\[ \overline{f(u)} = \sum_{i=1}^{N} p(u_i)\, f(u_i). \]
