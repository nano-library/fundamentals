Problem Sheet 2

Q1.

(a) Explain why leaving the refrigerator door open does not cool the kitchen.

(b) A reversible heat engine of thermal efficiency \( e = 0.60 \) operates between two reservoirs. The temperature of the cold reservoir is \( T_C = 300 \, \text{K} \). Determine the temperature of the hot reservoir \( T_H \). If the engine produces 300 J of mechanical work per cycle, determine the amount of heat rejected to the cold reservoir per cycle.

Q2.

(a) State the equipartition theorem.

(b) A rigid container of volume 1 L contains nitrogen gas (\( N_2 \)) at atmospheric pressure and room temperature. Determine the total internal (thermal) energy of the gas.

Q3.

A binary gas mixture at constant temperature and pressure contains two species of particles. Their chemical potentials are \( \mu_1 = 4 \times 10^{-21} \, \text{J}, \, \mu_2 = 6 \times 10^{-21} \, \text{J} \) respectively.

(a) If the system contains \( N_1 = 3.0 \times 10^{23} \) particles of species 1 and \( N_2 = 2.0 \times 10^{23} \) particles of species 2, calculate the total Gibbs free energy \( G \) of the mixture.

(b) If both \( N_1 \) and \( N_2 \) are doubled while keeping \( T \) and \( P \) fixed, does the Gibbs free energy double? Briefly justify your answer.

Q4.

Derive the thermodynamic identity for \( dF \) starting from the definition of the Helmholtz free energy,

\[F = U - TS.\]

Hence obtain expressions for the entropy \( S \), pressure \( P \), and chemical potential \( \mu \) in terms of the partial derivatives of \( F(T, V, N) \).

Q5.

Consider two distinguishable, non-interacting particles confined in identical one-dimensional infinite potential wells of length \( L \). The total energy of this two-particle system in terms of ground state energy \( E_1 \) is given by

\[E_{\text{tot}} = 10E_1.\]

Assume equal a priori probabilities and:

(a) List all accessible microstates \((n_1, n_2)\) consistent with the given total energy.

(b) Find the probability that particle 1 is in the ground state?

(c) Calculate the mean energy of particle 1.
