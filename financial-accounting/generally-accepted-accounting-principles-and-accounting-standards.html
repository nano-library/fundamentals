Generally Accepted Accounting Principles (GAAP) and Accounting Standards

In order to maintain uniformity and consistency in accounting records throughout the world, certain rules and principles have been developed which are generally accepted by the accounting profession. These rules/principles are known by different names such as principles, concepts, conventions, postulates and assumptions.

These rules are judged on their general acceptability rather than universal acceptability. Hence, they are popularly called Generally Accepted Accounting Principles (GAAP). The term “generally accepted” means that these principles have substantial support from professional accounting bodies.

GAAP refers to the rules or guidelines adopted for recording and reporting business transactions in financial statements. These principles have evolved over a long period of time on the basis of past experiences, usages and customs. They are also referred to as accounting concepts and conventions.

Accounting Standards

To promote worldwide uniformity in published accounts, the International Accounting Standards Committee (IASC) was set up in June 1973 with nine nations as founder members. The objective of this committee is to formulate and publish accounting standards in public interest, to be observed in the presentation of audited financial statements and to promote their worldwide acceptance.

The IASC works to reduce differences between accounting practices of different countries. This process of harmonisation helps users and preparers of financial statements to operate across international boundaries more effectively.

In India, the Institute of Chartered Accountants of India (ICAI) constituted the Accounting Standards Board (ASB) in 1977. The ASB has been empowered to formulate and issue accounting standards that are to be followed by business concerns in India.
