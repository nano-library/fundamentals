Financial Accounting - Introduction

It is concerned with recording of business transactions in the books of accounts in such a way that operating result of a particular period and financial position on a particular date can be known.

Basic Accounting Terms

Business : A business is an organization that produces and sells goods and services for profit.

Capital : Capital is the money and assets a business owner invests in the business. It is used to start, operate, and expand the business. It may also refer to the net worth of the business.

Drawings : Drawings are money or assets withdrawn by the owner from the business for personal use.

Goods : Goods are physical items that a business buys or sells. They may be raw materials, work-in-progress, or finished products.

Assets : An asset is a resource owned or controlled by a business that can generate economic value. Assets may be tangible or intangible and are recorded on the balance sheet.

Tangible Assets : Tangible assets are physical assets owned by a business that appear on the balance sheet, such as buildings, machinery, and inventory.

Intangible Assets : Intangible assets are non-physical assets owned by a business, such as goodwill, patents, trademarks, and copyrights.

Expenses : Expenses are the costs incurred by a business in the process of earning revenue.

Income : Income is the amount of money earned by an individual or business after deducting expenses and taxes.

Liability : Liabilities are the debts or financial obligations of a business payable to outsiders.

Users of Accounting Information

The basic objective of accounting is to provide information which is useful for persons and groups inside and outside the organisation.

I. Internal users: Internal users are those individuals or groups who are within the organisation like owners, management, employees and trade unions.

II. External users: External users are those individuals or groups who are outside the organisation like creditors, investors, banks and other lending institutions, present and potential investors, Government, tax authorities, regulatory agencies and researchers.

The users and their need for information are as follows:

Users Need for Information
Internal
i.  Owners To know the profitability and financial soundness of the business.
ii.  Management To take prompt decisions to manage the business efficiently.
iii.  Employees and Trade unions To form judgement about the earning capacity of the business since their remuneration and bonus depend on it.
External
i.  Creditors, banks and other lending institutions To determine whether the principal and the interest thereof will be paid in when due.
ii.  Present investors To know the position, progress and prosperity of the business in order to ensure the safety of their investment.
iii.  Potential investors To decide whether to invest in the business or not.
iv.  Government and Tax authorities To know the earnings in order to assess the tax liabilities of the business.
v.  Regulatory agencies To evaluate the business operation under the regulatory legislation.
vi.  Researchers To use in their research work.
