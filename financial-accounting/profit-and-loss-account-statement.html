Profit & Loss Account Statement

Statement of Profit & Loss Account

A Profit and Loss Statement is a financial statement used to outline income and expenses over a specific period. It is also called an Income Statement.

It is prepared to know the net profit or net loss incurred in a business during a particular period.

Problem 1

You are given the following information from the books of Anil Co. Ltd. as on 31-03-2024:

Particulars Amount
Material consumed 4,00,000
Opening stock 20,000
Salaries 67,000
Bonus to employees 10,000
Interest on loan 8,000
Depreciation on premises 4,000
Insurance 12,000
Depreciation on Machinery 9,000
Sales returns 20,000
Provision for tax 30,000
Machinery 3,00,000
P.F contribution 40,000
Premises 2,00,000
Sales 7,20,000
Equity Share Capital 4,00,000
Total 11,20,000

Additional Information: Closing stock was valued at Rs. 1,00,000. Prepare Statement of Profit & Loss account.

Answer:

Statement of Profit & Loss Account - Explanation (Anil Co. Ltd.)

1. Revenue from Operations

Sales = Rs. 7,20,000
Less: Sales Returns = Rs. 20,000
Net Revenue from Operations = Rs. 7,00,000

Explanation: Sales returns are deducted from total sales to arrive at Net Sales because returned goods do not generate revenue for the business.

2. Cost of Materials Consumed

Material Consumed = Rs. 4,00,000

Explanation: This represents the direct cost of raw materials used in production during the year.

3. Purchase of Stock-in-Trade

Closing Stock given = Rs. 1,00,000 Opening Stock = Rs. 20,000 Change in Stock = Opening - Closing = 20,000 - 1,00,000 = (80,000)

Explanation: Since Closing Stock is greater than Opening Stock, there is an increase in inventory. Increase in inventory is treated as a reduction in expense; therefore, it is shown as (80,000).

4. Employee Benefit Expenses

Salaries = Rs. 67,000
Bonus = Rs. 10,000
Provident Fund = Rs. 40,000
Total = Rs. 1,17,000

Explanation: All payments made to employees for services rendered during the year are grouped under employee benefit expenses.

5. Finance Costs

Interest on Loan = Rs. 8,000

Explanation: Interest paid on borrowed funds is treated as finance cost because it represents the cost of borrowing.

6. Depreciation & Amortization Expenses

Depreciation on Machinery = Rs. 9,000
Depreciation on Premises = Rs. 4,000
Total = Rs. 13,000

Explanation: Depreciation is charged to allocate the cost of fixed assets over their useful life.

7. Other Expenses

Insurance = Rs. 12,000

Explanation: Insurance is treated as a revenue expense because it relates to the current accounting period.

8. Total Expenses

Material Consumed = 4,00,000
Change in Inventory = (80,000)
Employee Benefits = 1,17,000
Finance Cost = 8,000
Depreciation = 13,000
Insurance = 12,000
----------------------------------
Total Expenses = Rs. 5,70,000

9. Profit Calculation

Total Revenue = Rs. 7,00,000
Less: Total Expenses = Rs. 5,70,000
Profit before Tax = Rs. 1,30,000
Less: Provision for Tax = Rs. 30,000
Profit after Tax = Rs. 1,00,000

Explanation: Profit before tax is calculated by deducting total expenses from total revenue. After deducting tax provision, the final Profit after Tax is Rs. 1,00,000.

Problem 2

From the following information, prepare Profit and Loss Account of ABC Co. Ltd. as on 31.03.2023.

Particulars Rs. Particulars Rs.
Excise Duty 8,000 Purchases 2,35,000
Provision for tax 10,000 Returns inward 5,000
Depreciation on Machinery 3,300 Closing stock 75,000
Sundry expenses 7,000 Opening stock 82,000
Rent 4,000 Wages 30,000
Salaries 7,500 Bank loan 40,000
Materials consumed 90,000 Interest on Bank loan 4,000
Machinery 25,000 Interest on Investment 5,000
Directors remuneration 20,000 Rent received 3,000
Factory expenses 2,500 Motive power 12,000
Sales 4,55,000 Transport Charges 1,000

Answer:

Revenue from Operations

Particulars Amount (Rs.)
Sales 455000
Less: Sales Returns (5000)
Less: Excise Duty (8000)
Total 442000

Other Income

Particulars Amount (Rs.)
Interest on Investments 5000
Rent Received 3000
Total 8000

Employee Benefit Expenses

Particulars Amount (Rs.)
Wages 30000
Salaries 7500
Total 37500

Other Expenses

Particulars Amount (Rs.)
Sundry Expenses 7000
Rent 4000
Directors’ Remuneration 20000
Factory Expenses 2500
Motive Power 12000
Transport Charges 1000
Total 46500

Explanation

1. Revenue from Operations

Sales amounted to Rs. 4,55,000. From this, Sales Returns (Returns Inward) of Rs. 5,000 and Excise Duty of Rs. 8,000 are deducted. Excise duty is treated as a reduction from revenue when it is included in sales figures.

Net Revenue from Operations = 4,55,000 − 5,000 − 8,000 = Rs. 4,42,000.

2. Other Income

Income not arising from the core business operations is treated as Other Income. Here:

  • Interest on Investments = Rs. 5,000
  • Rent Received = Rs. 3,000

Total Other Income = 5,000 + 3,000 = Rs. 8,000.

3. Employee Benefit Expenses

Expenses related to employees are grouped together:

  • Wages = Rs. 30,000
  • Salaries = Rs. 7,500

Total Employee Benefit Expense = 30,000 + 7,500 = Rs. 37,500.

4. Other Expenses

Operating and administrative expenses are classified under Other Expenses:

  • Sundry Expenses = Rs. 7,000
  • Rent = Rs. 4,000
  • Directors’ Remuneration = Rs. 20,000
  • Factory Expenses = Rs. 2,500
  • Motive Power = Rs. 12,000
  • Transport Charges = Rs. 1,000

Total Other Expenses = 7,000 + 4,000 + 20,000 + 2,500 + 12,000 + 1,000 = Rs. 46,500.

These calculated figures are then used in preparing the Statement of Profit and Loss to determine Profit before Tax and Profit after Tax.
