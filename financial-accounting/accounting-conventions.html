Accounting Conventions

Accounting Conventions

An accounting convention refers to common practices which are universally followed in recording and presenting accounting information of a business entity. They are followed like customs and traditions. Accounting conventions help in maintaining uniformity and consistency in financial reporting and in comparing accounting data of different business units or of the same unit for different periods.

  • Convention of Consistency
  • Convention of Full Disclosure
  • Convention of Materiality
  • Convention of Conservatism

1. Convention of Consistency

This convention means that the same accounting principles and methods should be used year after year in preparing financial statements. Meaningful comparisons can be made only when accounting policies and practices are followed in a uniform and consistent manner over a period of time. If different procedures are adopted in different years, the results will not be comparable.

2. Convention of Full Disclosure

According to this convention, all material and relevant facts concerning financial statements should be fully disclosed. Adequate means sufficient disclosure of information to enable users such as investors, lenders, creditors and shareholders to take informed decisions. Financial statements should present all relevant information in a fair and complete manner.

3. Convention of Materiality

The convention of materiality states that only material facts, that is, information which influences the decisions of users, should be disclosed in financial statements. Unimportant or immaterial information may be omitted or merged with other items.

4. Convention of Conservatism

This convention is based on the principle “anticipate no profit, but provide for all possible losses.” It suggests that while recording transactions, expected losses should be provided for, but anticipated profits should not be recorded until they are actually realized. For example, while valuing stock, cost price or market price whichever is lower is considered.
